Dora Violet Colquhoun

About Me

Dora Colquhoun is an energetic and enthusiastic neurodiverse performer, devisor and facilitator based in Liverpool. She recently completed a Masters in Applied Theatre with practices in criminal justice at Royal Central School of Speech and Drama (2019).

During her masters Dora worked with Haringey Council leading a project in care homes across Tottenham with participants about place and identity. Dora also facilitated on a violence prevention course with Unlocked Theatre at Liverpool HMP. In June 2019 Dora had the dream opportunity to train on 4 month residency in Vermont USA with not for profit theatre organisation Bread and Puppet.

In 2018, Dora worked as a Mediator for the Liverpool Biennial, designing bespoke workshops and tours at St Georges Hall, The Bluecoat, and Liverpool Playhouse responding to the Biennial theme ‘Beautiful World Where Are You?.’

Dora was commissioned by the Liverpool Biennial to create the event ‘Beautiful woman where are you?’ a series of talks collaborating with local female artists to discuss their experience of being a woman in the arts.

Dora has a background in facilitation teaching in Croatia with TRY (The Rijeka Youth Theatre) in 2017. Dora has extensive experience working in children’s theatre having toured intensively for 6 months across Italy with Theatrino theatre (2016)

In 2014 Dora completed the Hope Street Emerging Artist Program an intensive acting program devising and performing outdoor and site specific work. Dora Collaborated with Tate Liverpool, Liverpool Council, Adam McGuigan and Barefeet theatre.

Dora has wealth of varied experience as a theatre maker and entertainer she graduated from Dartington College of Arts in 2011 with a first class BA Hons in Contemporary Performance Art Combining Community Practices.

Since graduating from Dartington she has been influenced by their progressive inter disciplinary teaching methods. Dora has always strived to collaborate with other artists/experts from a variety of disciplines as she feels this adds dimensions and an extension of knowledge to the work.

Alongside being an actor she has a passion for devising, song writing and physical comedy having completed workshops with Performers Playground (2018), collaborating with Mark Winstanley and Alice Robinson learning Bouffon clown practices.

Dora is full of creative knowledge and an extremely versatile performer. She has worked with Cbeebies, Action Transport Theatre, Wild Rumpus, and Coco Loco.

Shows

The project ADHD The Musical: Can I Have Your Attention Please? Was born from my personal experience being assessed for ADHD. At the age of 30, this is considered a late diagnosis; not that I was late for my diagnosis, I made sure I was on time!

I sat with the information that I had ADHD for a year like a mother duck sitting on an egg. Then one day whilst cycling along the Liverpool to Leeds canal I decided I needed a neuroscientist!

I contacted neuroscientist Dr Joydeep Bhattacharya and began having conversations with him about the brain. My conversations with Dr Joy inspired me to create a musical performance lecture. I combined my love of musical theatre and Ted Talks to make an experience that was entertaining, informative and heartfelt.

The piece ADHD The Musical explores the highs and lows of having the most common neuro-developmental disorder worldwide alongside good old fashioned storytelling, singing, and dancing with inspiration from legends Julie Andrews, Dolly and Cher.

The show attempts to unpack feelings of shame and blame that occur when being neuro- divergent in a world designed for the neuro typical brain.

I am delighted to be awarded by the Arts Council funding 2021 and be supported Dadafest. I am looking for places, festivals and venues to showcase the piece.

The Flowerpot Women

Funded by the Liverpool Culture Without Walls Fund (2020) Performed at Bluecoat Garden devised in collaboration with Izzie Major and Jonathan McGuire. First developed in collaboration with On The Verge Festival 2015

Flowerpot women a dark absurd comedy described as "Becket mixed with french and Saunders on acid’ about the pitfalls of Eddie and Garlands time in the garden together. A charming absurd piece about friendship, fauna and “getting on”

We Are All Stardust 2020

Collective Encounters commissioned 10 new works by emerging artists. The commissions formed part of their Above & Beyond project, and responded to themes of “community power” and “community action”.

Creative responses took a variety of forms from illustration, poetry, short films and music.

Dora Colquhoun and Jonathan McGuire created a collaborative music piece exploring The Overview Effect; a theory used to describe the awe inspired feeling astronauts experience when viewing the Earth from Space.
